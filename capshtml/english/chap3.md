## A Man's Shame


<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Wait!" Ciao pleaded. "What did I do!? W-Who are you!?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Don't play dumb! Son of a bitch," the man shouted, bringing a hand to his head as if it hurt. "Leave my fucking things there."</p>

—What were the odds!? Pox, do not mess with me.

Turns out that backpack had indeed been placed there deliberately, and apparently the owner had just shown up.

Observing the situation from another perspective, it was so unfortunately specific and ill-timed, with one of the cubes still in his hand, he couldn't make a credible excuse, and even if he could, the person in front of him didn't seem like the type to listen and dialogue.
Even returning it right then, it was unlikely he'd get out unharmed. Everything pointed down the same path.

Ciao didn't know how to fight, at least that's what his body told him. He wasn't sure if he'd ever done it but his arms refused to prepare. His legs trembled and responded poorly to what he wanted to do.

Clumsy, pathetic as if walking on oiled ice with his hands tied, he tried to stand up.
The man's face was disturbing, Ciao knew those were tattoos but it gave the illusion that he had no eyes, so no matter how much courage he tried to gather, his body was being carried away by the panic of the moment.
If that tank didn't have those tattoos, his fear would be reduced by at least nine point twelve percent. Maybe.

As he rose slowly, he put his hands in front to protect himself.

—Pox, and now what do I d—

"Huh?"

In an instant, he was looking at the man's shoes in front of him. He didn't understand what happened. His mind didn't want to understand, it focused more on the worn but fresh and innovative-looking footwear.

Everything went black. —What? The lights had gone out again, for a few seconds.

When he recovered from his dazed state, he realized he was lying on the ground.

He looked up and saw a skull screaming at him, but his buzzing ears wouldn't let him understand a single word. He assumed they were insults and part of him felt relieved not to understand, since words caused him oh so much pain.

He observed the man's face and found it disgusting how he spat when speaking. He noticed the tattoos and knew some were text but obviously couldn't read any. He wore a blue and black checkered shirt, and grayish shorts; his eyes moved from side to side, trembling occasionally.

He tried to stand up once more.

He saw the same shoe of the man, this time in more detail. He even appreciated the stitches that were evidently made with care and patience. He quite liked the design.
The material was also of good quality despite the irreversible wear, they were high shoes, white and very eye-catching, plus his socks were also white, and covered his entire ankle.

All that right next to his face.

And then... black. A flash of light. Black. Light. The sky, stars. The walls he already had memorized. The floor again.

His head slammed furiously against the ground, his right cheek scraped and dirtied with mud and gravel, he felt very clearly his brain bouncing in his skull and his mind returned. His ears popped open suddenly and his pupils spun out of control.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"—!... say anything!? Bitch ass?"</p>


He adjusted his cap and rolled his back backward to see the man, who kept approaching, leaving Ciao with less and less space. He blinked like crazy nonstop, his eyes couldn't focus properly.

He felt something, wet, warm, enter his mouth, it tasted disgusting. He touched it and it was a thick liquid, red. Both his nose and his gums were bleeding, a lot.

It had only been a few hours since he woke up and he hadn't thought about fighting until this moment, now when he understood he had received at least one hit, he felt confused since even without remembering receiving any before this day, it seemed obvious to expect that a blow like that would hurt too much.
In his case, Ciao had felt the impacts clearly but wouldn't say they had been painful.

He spat the blood and this time managed to stand up. He looked at the man, heard what he claimed, but didn't understand anything.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"What are you staring at me for!? Asshole. Worthless thief"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"W-Wait... Wait, wait, wait." Ciao begged, lowering his head while protecting himself with one hand and wiping the blood from his lips using the other "Please, wai-..."</p>

Plwagh-...

He was interrupted by a fist the size of three quarters of his head; so powerfully that it made him turn his head and spit more of the blood accumulated in his mouth.

The only thing he could think about was how ridiculous his face looked, with his mouth open, dripping blood and saliva.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Pox!! Wait!, What is happening!?" Ciao screamed desperately, almost wanting to cry— I already left it, pox! I am not stealing anything!"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Don't yell at me, you bitch ass! Who d'ya think you are?" He pressed his forehead with both hands, speaking with difficulty—. W-What the fuck are you doing here? Who sent you!?"</p>

Ciao's mind was spinning, everything was happening so fast that he couldn't even force himself to make a theory. He felt some teeth moving more than they should and got frustrated imagining losing one. 

—Pox. Pox. What is happening? I am scared. Why? What is this place? Why did Vicente bring me here? I am scared. Was it a trap? Pox. I am a fucking coward. Pox. Pox. My clothes are already covered in blood. Damn. I hate this. Why have they hit me? I don't know how to fight, what am I going to do? Do not fuck with me! Help. I am scared. I do not want to do this. What do I do? What do I do? What do I do? What do I do?

—!

Then a single thought untied all the knots in his brain. His heart pounded violently. His chest was boiling and his body recovered some will. Everything in that single instant.
A feeling of hope. The mere idea made the sky no longer look so dark and, although in the hours he'd spent not even a sexual thought had crossed his mind, what he felt certainly made something harden in his groin. He was almost completely sure. Only a small part of him doubted, but the evidence couldn't be denied.

—All that before... It did not hurt.

His face and head had hit the ground, he had received at least two kicks, his nose was bleeding and his lip and mouth too. However, despite all that, it didn't hurt. He didn't feel anything beyond the blood flowing.

Then he understood, or so he thought. 

—Could it be that...

He prepared in the closest thing he could think of to a fighting pose. His arms looked like branches, they didn't compare to the tattooed trunks in front of him. But there was hope. 
He didn't want to fight, he didn't understand why he had to do it, he didn't think he could do it. But there was nothing else, even someone like him, however minimal, had his pride, he wasn't going to let himself be beaten up without trying to defend himself.

He adjusted his cap that luckily hadn't fallen off and ran with all his strength and soul toward the man, raised his weak left fist, without technique, without experience.

—It is possible that I have become...

He wasn't even a meter away, but he felt like he ran for kilometers full of determination.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Go mess with yourself, son of a...!"</p>

His steps were stupid, heavy, but sure.
When he was close enough, he put all his courage into the blow and threw it.

However, before impacting, the giant in front of him, hit him full in the face. It was obvious he had real fighting experience, Ciao couldn't even putting all his speed make the slightest contact.
But even so, he didn't retreat, he received the blow with something similar to joy; he wouldn't have been able to dodge it anyway, but he wasn't trying either. He was clinging to that formless idea that appeared in his head.

His neck bent backward, making his bones sound grotesquely. He tasted the man's fist. It disgusted him.

Although his incompetence seemed obvious, part of him didn't lose hope of landing that blow. Still he wasn't disappointed since that was as useful. His mind still couldn't form a detailed theory, he felt too dizzy to think clearly, but he felt he had got something.

The blow crushed his nose and his face looked ridiculous, that worried him. But the relief was greater since he confirmed his idea, intentionally accepting the impact and, as expected, it didn't hurt at all. That encouraged him but it seemed a little hard to believe, so he decided to trust the irrefutable, and what more than his own experience.
That was Ciao's way, to cling to the provable.

—Yes! It did not hurt, it did not hurt at all. Nothing, pox, it did not hurt at all! It really seems that I have gained more... more...

The man, who didn't expect to see someone do something so stupid, couldn't help stopping from confusion, it was only a second. But it was enough. 

Ciao hit, hit and hit with all his strength, three times. His shameful thread-like arm gave everything it had, he didn't hold back and threw three punches that while they hit, were as encouraging as humiliating, exposing that he possessed no fighting skill despite being a man.

He didn't want to look around, though rather than to concentrate on his opponent, it was because he didn't want to even imagine what he would feel if someone else were watching his stupidity.

He looked at the living skull he had hit. He looked irritated, desperate, one of his eyes closed involuntarily and he pressed his mouth hard, some tears formed in those black eyes.
<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Son of a... bitch." His expression turned to one of hatred and with just a gesture, Ciao knew he was putting all his strength. Then, he leaned slightly backward and charged a kick that, for the blond weakling's body, was even stronger than when he crashed into Vicente earlier.</p>

Ciao coughed and breathed hard, but didn't fall, he bent his legs and extended his arms to balance himself, and managed to stay standing. He scraped his palms on the floor, but didn't let himself be thrown down. He inhaled deeply and blushed tenderly because he felt a little proud of himself.

—Oh, do not mess with me...

The person in front of him, however, was already sick of him. From his pants pockets, he pulled out a large folding knife. He grabbed it hard and lunged at Ciao. He even hated himself for wasting time with that underweight idiot.

Ciao was unprepared, he didn't expect him to have a weapon, worse still, that this would be reason to use it. He stayed still for a moment and the man didn't stop. But Ciao didn't cower, something inside him gave him the courage to continue. He even felt like he was another person; it didn't matter, he had already thought about what he would do next.

The bone head, adjusted his balance, putting one foot forward toward Ciao, faster than a blink, took momentum and his left hand, which held the knife, shot toward him, following an arched trajectory.

Even so, Ciao had his plan, and so he achieved it. The blade cut his shirt and scratched his abdomen but, it wasn't fatal, since he was already carrying out his strategy.
Which consisted of giving a slight reverse jump, arching his body in the shape of letter *c*. This however, didn't go as expected, after all, an imbecile without technique can't do something like that without prior training.

Instead, he lost balance and fell to the ground, hitting his ass and lower back. His shirt was left open by the cut and his abdomen dripped.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"A-Ah... Pox! Go to hell! Go to hell!"</p>

He leaned on his back and elbows, and kicked his legs like a bitch to, if lucky, kick the man's hand and make him drop the knife.
Of course, that was only possible in his mind. The giant stopped both his legs, grabbing them together from the pants, with a single hand.

The lamp kept darkening everything at moments.

Everything felt so slow, he observed the man's face, not only was it tattooed but also, he didn't have the soft part of the nose, it was practically skin stuck to bone. A tear fell from his right eye, which seemed to convulse, trembling and closing on its own.

With Ciao on the ground and no possibility of getting up, he took momentum and launched his hand with the knife toward a soft and weak chicken neck.

—And what did I do? pox... Pox. Do not fuck with me. I am a fucking clown. I could not even land those hits properly. I am going to die right here. Stupid homeless man.

Ciao watched the knife approach, his brain searched for anything to save him, he saw the broken lamp; he saw the tattoos on the hand holding the knife, couldn't recognize anything much less read what seemed to be short phrases; anything, whatever, anything, anything, anything, anything.

—What an imbecile, how fucking imbecile I am. So stupid... Pox, Pox. Damn. This idiot is surely laughing at me. I look so ridiculous.... Pox. I always make a fucking fool of myself.

He stopped putting force, he let himself fall backward, his head hit the ground so hard he thought it would break. But it didn't matter anymore. He was ready to die.

—Anyway something like this, so full of shame and humiliation cannot be called a life, could it? And if really so much time passed as they told me... But I was resisting... What is the use? What was I going to do anyway? Do not fuck with me... I truly am the lowest kind of coward... I am scared.

On the ground, it felt like an eternity. He just wanted everything to end already, he saw the knife that didn't stop but didn't complete the task either. It went so slow, but it wasn't just that knife, it was him too. His body couldn't move either, only his mind and eyes had acquired impressive speed.

Then with the corner of his eye, he saw someone, it wasn't the skull, it wasn't Vicente. Not far, in the middle of darkness and light, behind a collapsed wall, hid a girl with black hair. He directed his gaze to search for hers.

—...N-No. Do nott fuck with me. No. ¡Damn!

Bandages could be seen on her hands and she held something that looked like a paint roller, also bandaged. Ciao looked at her, she was shocked, probably had been watching the "fight" for some time.—How much time passed?
Her pupils were large and youthful, although she was one hundred percent human, her eyes gave a more feline appearance than Ciao's own. The girl cut the moment when she looked higher up.

This was the maximum humiliation. Damn, being brutally beaten in public or in alone was already something that made him want to cry from shame. But a girl, and of an age similar to his... Watching him, being reduced to what he really was, giving him that look of pity and disappointment that he knew well.
If she had been watching his attempt at defense, surely she had been laughing at him for a good while.

—No, pox, this cannot repeat.

The fire in his chest was weak, he felt it fading little by little as he remembered how short this new life had been. He recapped everything from the beginning, and the moment he uncovered the container in which he woke up, stayed in his mind. Since that time, he got up with all the power he could and smashed his head against the lid to open it.

Do not mock me. Please. —What do I do?— Really, please do not look at me.

When he got down from that house, he fell, and it hadn't been a short height, he impacted fully on the ground, He even felt his arm bend.

I have to impress her. Them.

—But even so....

He was bitten by dogs and then hit by a cart at full speed. And his body continued as if it was nothing.

—The truth is that the future is not as disappointing as I thought, right?

Besides that, just a few moments ago, he received blows from the immense man, one after another. Then a knife cut his trunk... But it was a superficial cut.... And more than that...

—...I have become—

And, without thinking, almost unconscious, he put all his weight on his right arm, since didn't pass one hundred and twenty two pounds, it wasn't so difficult. He gathered what remained of his strength, he couldn't allow this humiliation. 
What mattered now was to not embarrass himself, not looking pathetic in front of the girl.

His eyes opened to their maximum capacity. He felt the girl's gaze and time became fast again, actually not even five seconds had passed. At that moment, the lights went out.

And then...

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"I have become strong!"</p>

A grotesque sound was heard, some soft material being pierced by something.

A white and delicate face, in front of a rough and black one, a weak hand and another that seemed impenetrable, neither could see each other, but at that moment, both felt a thick liquid splash their faces.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"!"</p>
