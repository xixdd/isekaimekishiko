
## The Curiosity That Killed the Cat

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Hey, hey! Get outta here... Leave 'im alone."</p>

It was a voice, deep, too deep, and that alone was enough to make Ciao feel even more pathetic than he already was.

Stumbling with a beer can in each hand, a street bum kicked and scattered the pack of dogs that had been attacking Ciao. There wasn't much resistance, which perhaps said something about how well those two already knew each other.

—Damn it all.

After the commotion had settled, trembling with fear, Ciao rose slowly to his feet and raised one arm in front of himself as a shield against the man.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"I am sorry, I am so sorry. It-It was not on purpose!" —he clarified, already crouching to gather the scattered junk from the ground, eyes deliberately away from the man.</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"You crazy or somethin', foo? Defend yourself... Look how they left ya."</p>

The moment the man finished that sentence, Ciao pressed both hands to his mouth and his eyes flew wide open. A combination of stenches, rot, earth, filth, urine, and mostly alcohol, all filled Ciao's nose entirely, and he chose to swallow his own vomit so as to avoid yet another humiliation.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"These dogs... they smell fear, foo. Act tough."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Huh? Yes, well... It is that—"</p>

When he lifted his gaze, something made him stop. On top of the vagrant, on his head. An animal, was it?

No. But close.

It was his hairstyle. Shaved on both sides, and at the crown his hair had been cut and arranged into the shape of a rodent; well, "arranged" made it sound considerably cleaner than what it actually was. Down the nape of his neck hung a dirty braid, apparently meant to simulate a tail.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Pretty wild, huh?" He flashed a yellow grin, wiggling both eyebrows and pointing at his own head.</p>

Ciao's brow furrowed and one eye twitched with revulsion.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Oh, well then. In any case, I am sorry... m-man. As you can see, what these streets are like, my cart got away from me."</p>

In that, the vagrant was not wrong, especially in this particular stretch. It was full of bumps, slopes, and exaggerated depressions. The very definition of rough terrain.

—Do not mess with me, this man is rancid —His nose was burning.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Umm, yes. I am sorry too, in any case."</p>

After some seconds, he finished putting everything back in place, though it was already clear the cart had exceeded its capacity long before this.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Well, I think that is everything. Apologies once ag—"</p>

The man was staring at Ciao very intently. Intently enough that it had become uncomfortable. A shiver ran down the full length of his spine, causing a spasm of disgust.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Mhm..." He cleared his throat "But look at how those bastards left ya, foo."</p>

The sleeves of his shirt were soaked in blood. He rolled them up to inspect the wounds; nothing irreparable, but not something to be ignored either. The strange thing was that, despite all appearances, he did not feel even the slightest pain or discomfort.

He had never been bitten by a dog before —nor any other animal, for that matter— so he could not say with certainty. But most people would assume that sort of thing ought to hurt. And much more so, given the blood.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Those damn dogs are real territorial" he explained, adjusting his cart. "I already whooped 'em before. Back when I had a black belt in a buncha martial arts, they barely gave me any real trouble."</p>

As he said this, he attempted to demonstrate with fighting moves that were far too advanced for a drunk man's balance to support.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Come on, foo. I know a guy around here who can fix ya up."</p>

He said it with such confidence that it sounded as though they had known each other for years already.

The other thought about it for a moment. The man was a complete disaster, he reeked of death, and Ciao did not know him at all... Even so, he did not know anyone else either. And if this man took him to a doctor, he might be able to obtain information from someone more qualified.

Moreover, the vagrant had not reacted with violence when Ciao knocked over his belongings. So dangerous, he probably was not.

In truth, even if all of that were partially accurate,  Ciao was simply making excuses to avoid admitting that saying no was something he did not know how to do.

And so, he allowed himself to be led away…


<p align="center">❈❈❈❈❈❈❈❈</p>



The man introduced himself as Vicente while the two of them walked side by side. For the second time, Ciao had to force down his own vomit as a blackened, grime-covered hand came to rest against his back.

Several minutes passed in silence,  well, not entirely. There was also the sound of the filthy man's hiccups, echoing steadily.

Vicente moved with confidence despite being drunk. That he knew these streets thoroughly, that much was clear. After all, they were where he came from.

Or rather, that was the impression Ciao had received: that the man walking beside him was a vagrant and a drifter. He wore black trousers thick with dirt; a jacket that might once have been for winter, now so worn it looked made of paper; also black, with dirt and other filth stuck to it.

But come to think about it...

—Have I too become a vagrant?!

Vicente at least had his *junk and stuff*, which he could and intended to sell later. Ciao, on the other hand, did not have even the smallest resource to his name. Seen that way, Ciao's situation was the worse one.

Well, technically speaking, Ciao did have a home to return to, and surely someone was waiting there for him to eat and sleep...

—Is that so?

Perhaps it was not entirely accurate to say someone was waiting for him there. In fact,he had not received a single letter from them in a very long time.

Because despite being an only child, the kind of son that parents want close to them forever,  that was not what he was. He was the kind of son one watches from a distance, sighing, thinking: "Where did it go wrong?" Even if he could honestly say that they loved him unconditionally, it was undeniable that at least a couple of times they would have felt disappointed by what their first and only descendant had turned out to be.

And on top of that, how much time had passed since he had been away, or how far away he was. He had no idea.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Excuse me... By any chance, today's newspaper, would you happen to have it? Umm, Vicente."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Today's? Well that depends on what day it is today, foo" He let out a dry laugh that became a cough "Look, this one, far as I know it ain't that old."</p>

Vicente pulled out one of the cleaner newspapers from his cart, shook it out, and gave it a glance.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Yeah, look... Seventh, hmm, July... Two thousand eighteen. Yeah, I think it's recent."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"You cannot be seri—"</p>

His feet stopped so suddenly that the momentum made his whole body sway. His lungs empty, he turned his head to the right and looked at Vicente. His jaw hung loose.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Hey, sorry, dude... It ain't like I can afford to buy it every day, y'know?"</p>

—Two thousand eighteen?! What in the pox is this? Pox!

Ciao felt something crush his chest in that second; squeezing, twisting, without mercy. A knot formed in his throat, so large that even the mere thought of words tore him apart from the inside.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"I-Is that... the date? Are you cert—"</p>

No... ¿...How-...?

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Well, yeah. Says so right here..."</p>

Two faces appeared in his mind. His voice cracked.

And Mother and my Father...

—No… No, no.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"And I did not even— And I-I did not even, I never once said—"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"You... doin' alright, foo?"</p>

His red pupils shrank even further. His face was frozen; directed toward Vicente, but not actually seeing him.

And then... I—...

It was not possible that they were still alive. If the date was true, if the year truly was two thousand eighteen, then this was a future far more distant than what he had calculated. At least eighty-seven years had passed. That would make more sense, considering how advanced the technology was.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"How is it that...?"</p>

—Then why is my body as old?!

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"...foo?"</p>

He could not believe it, especially coming from this revolting man. There was no way that much time could have passed.

—And that stupid newspaper, I cannot even read it. Pox!!

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Hey, I'm talkin' to ya, dude!" Vicente exclaimed, waving his filthy hands in front of Ciao's face.</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Ah! Shut the f—"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"..."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Oh, I—. He scratched his head, exasperated. "I am sorry, all right. Let us just leave it at that, please. I do not have time for this. I need to go. Now."</p>

—Calm down. Do not jump to conclusions yet. This man cannot be trusted... Yes, that is it. Do not think about it... About... Them.

He was walking fast to burn off the stress, though the vagrant's slow pace was killing him, he was not bold enough to leave without the last word.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Man, I was just tryin' to help" He clicked his tongue. "I'm drunk, sure, but not lying. That paper's like five days old, tops."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"I-I will not be able to continue accompanying you. It will be dark soon, and where I am going to spend the night, I do not even know that."</p>

—Pox. Why did I let him know my situation?

He had already turned at an angle, ready to run the moment it was possible.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"S'alright then" He accepted, stroking the rat atop his head. "But do me a, yeah, foo? I'm already late to sell this stuff. And I helped ya out earlier."</p>

— And I helped pick up your garbage, did I not!? he thought, dragging both hands down his face.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Come on, whatever ya got, foo. Just a few coins. I'm runnin' real dry and the hangover's gonna hit hard..."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Wh-What? I am sorry" He said, waving a hand. "The truth is, not a single *wo*, I do not have one. Yes, I apologize..."</p>

Vicente's face lost every last trace of emotion. It hardened, darkened, and dried; elongating so much it might have touched the floor.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Oh, wait!" He remembered, and began searching in his chest pocket, which was tied around his waist now. "This is the only thing I have. I do not use it. Perhaps it will be of use to you?"</p>

He simply wanted to end everything and get out of there. The sunlight was already fading and they were still in the middle of a dusty stretch of nowhere. For that reason, he decided to offer the dark rectangle with its reflective surface.

— The personal cinema device, right?

He extended his hand. Vicente looked even more disappointed and rejected it with a wave.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Nah, dude. What'm I supposed to do with your phone? People'll think I stole it, can't even sell it."</p>

—Phone, is it?... This time I was correct. As it turns out, its value is rather mediocre.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"And... s-so?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Well... just forget it, then... Dang."</p>

Ciao gave a nod, and seeing Vicente do the same, he finally began to walk away.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Hey, foo!"</p>

—What in the pox does he want now!!?

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"No money, so where ya gonna stay? The sidewalk ain't as comfy as it sounds, y'know?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"That is..." He scratched his cheek with one finger, trying to produce a smile."Well, umm..."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Look, come on, dude. I know someone who can help ya out. And before ya say anything he not a... drinker. I know that's what bothers ya."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"..."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"And it won't cost ya nothin', foo. Think about it, you ain't gonna find one deal better that this one. Yeah?"</p>

In the next *frame*, Ciao was already at his side, ready to continue the journey.

<p align="center">❈❈❈❈❈❈❈❈</p>

What remained was only the light of the street lamps, and what leaked from houses and a few businesses. Since Ciao had woken up in this city,  in this country, about three hours had passed already.

By now they knew each other somewhat better, having talked along the way. Ciao decided to trust the man and explained his situation simply, omitting the temporal displacement. In short, he claimed to be a tourist who had strayed from his group.

The conversation was fluid. Ciao felt more at ease now that he had a *acquaintance*, in quotation marks. There had even been some shared laughter.

Looking at the dried blood on Ciao's sleeves, it was evident the wounds had closed. He had not disinfected them, or even rinsed them off.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Look, foo." Vicente pulled a small bag from his pocket; inside were a few tablets and capsules. "If it starts hurtin', take one of these... But don't go takin' 'em all at once, seriously, wouldn't recommend it."</p>

Ciao took the bag and put it away in his chest rig. Even now, he had not felt the slightest discomfort. But turning down things offered to him was not something he knew how to do.

By this point, the effects of the alcohol in Vicente had nearly worn off, that much was visible.

They were sitting outside a small shop with a transparent front that displayed all its products to anyone passing by. Vicente had managed to sell part of his junk, and they shared some bread rolls he bought. Ciao offered him all the water he had left.

The pale light of the shop lit their backs as they stared into nothing.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"And those signs over there... criminals, are they? At such a young age?" Ciao asked, pointing at some posters with children's photos stuck to the glass behind them. They had text he could not read, but they resembled the kind that offer rewards for turning in fugitives.</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"No, foo, come on, haha. They're missing kids..."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"...Oh."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"... Don't tell me where ya come from it's so fancy that people don't go missing, huh?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"No, well, yes, but umm... They are not announced like this"</p>

Involuntarily, every one of those faces became burned into his memory.

They finished their bread and got ready to keep moving. According to Vicente, they were only a few minutes away.

The one with cat ears shook off his hands and trousers as he stood. The other simply took his nearly empty cart and they continued walking.

At such a late hour, even fewer people and sounds remained in the streets, to the point where Ciao could hear the heartbeat of the man beside him. Except it was actually just Vicente imitating the sound. Ciao looked at him with a wrinkled nose, and the other simply let out a stupid laugh.

At last they arrived at a *T*-shaped intersection. The part where all paths met was the only illuminated area; a yellowish lamp flickered, plunging everything into complete darkness at intervals. Once standing there, everything else was near-absolute black: the road they had come from and both options ahead. There was also more garbage than usual,  piles of it. Construction debris and overturned dumpsters.

Vicente seemed nervous, glancing at Ciao from the corner of his eye. Neither said anything. The only sound was the electric hum of the lamp.

—Not this man staring at me again!?

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Umm, is something wrong, Vicente?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Huh? Oh, nah. Nah, foo" He forced a smile, bringing a hand to his chin. "Look... just put this on, yeah?"</p>

From his cart he reached in again and produced a cloth bag. He opened it and pulled out a red cap, one that looked far too well-kept and clean to possibly belong to him. He offered it to Ciao.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Look, honestly I don't mind what ya prefer, alright? But the truth is, around here, better if ya put this on, foo."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Ahh, understood, I suppose. Why is that?"</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Well, y'know how it is, dude. I figure ya ain't gonna take your accessories off, right? And also your hai— But it ain't about me! Okay!?"</p>

From the very beginning Ciao had been about to put the cap on, but the vagrant kept stopping him.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"It's just... those, y'know" He avoided Ciao's eyes and pointed at his head. "Your little... ears."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Pardon?" He blinked several times, his face going red at the way Vicente had said it. "I see. If it is those you are referring to... Umm, taking them off... I do not think I am able to do that."</p>

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Yeah, yeah, I know. I respect your... preferences an'... all that... That's exactly why I'm sayin' it. But at least put the cap on for now, just to cover 'em up."</p>

Vicente breathed heavily. Even under the grime and his dark complexion, it was visible that he had gone red in the face. Finally he exhaled hard and made his decision.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"How do I put this... Look, there are some who might, uh... Get upset, yeah. Seeing someone who is... like that, it upsets them."</p>

—Like that.

Ciao understood everything, or so he believed. It was true. Someone like that, even he himself had felt revulsion toward such a person. Somehow he had forgotten it for a few hours, but it was real.

—If they see my ears, they will as about... About my... Tail.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Okay look I know you're here but hold on don't put it on yet lemme find someone first yeah okay don't move I'll be right back okay bye"</p>

He said it rapidly, without pausing. Then ran off.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"If someone comes, put it on!"</p>

And like that, Ciao was left alone. Watching as a man with a rat atop his head, pushing a rattling cart, was swallowed up by the darkness of the night. The truth is, it was not a rat, it was his hairstyle.


<p align="center">❈❈❈❈❈❈❈❈</p>



Instantly, his nose felt relieved. Even after all those hours, he had never gotten used to that man's stench. He stretched both arms upward and sighed deeply.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Ahhhh.... Yes, what a rel—"</p>

—Pox, He said—...!!

Vicente's words would not stop echoing in his head. Around here, were people really that aggressive? Despite hating the fact that he had no tail, it was not a hatred extreme enough to make him turn to violence.

Or perhaps it was simply directed at anyone who was not an ordinary human. Every person he had seen, men, women, young, old: none of them had animal ears or a tail. That would make considerably more sense.

Quickly he put on the cap, making sure his ears were well hidden. He fixed his hair so that the gap where human ears would normally be was not visible.

Then he sat down on a large chunk of rubble.

Everything was so quiet.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"!!"</p>

This time, the heartbeat he could hear was his own. His breathing became difficult as his own thoughts invaded him.

He turned red and felt his chest wanting to break open.

—Pox. What is this? It is not possible that I am in the future. No. No.
No, it is simply not possible, it should not be possible. How could it—? And why me of all people—? And so many years, there is no way this could have happened... But it is true. It is happening. Damn it all.

And... And now... What in the pox am I supposed to do? Waiting for this man, what am I doing that for?... And...

And my....

—No... How is it that—?

My parents...

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Shut up, idiot. Breathe. The date is not yet confirmed. Yes, stop thinking about that. Finding somewhere to spend the night, that is what matters right now..."</p>

And so more time passed in waiting, his vision flickering on and off thanks to the lamp to his left.

The only thing keeping him composed, keeping him from falling into panic, was observing the architecture. Especially in this area, where the buildings were taller. The cubes had been incredible, but these prism-shaped structures were even more so. Truly efficient, placing one house on top of another, and another, and one more still. Space was saved, and the silhouette was imposing.

Whether this new appreciation for architecture had developed just now, or whether it came from those years that only his body had lived through; he wondered.

Then...

—Do not think about it, do not think about it...

About fifteen minutes passed and there were no more buildings left to observe. The lighting simply did not reach any further.

He turned around looking for something to distract himself with, but there was only garbage. Disappointed, he had no choice but to observe that too, searching for anything to keep his mind occupied.

That is when he saw it:

Something stood out too conspicuously to ignore. A black bag, its contrast with the surrounding garbage was unmistakable even from a distance, and was obvious that someone had placed it there intentionally.

Ciao approached the pile of garbage and crouched down to open the backpack carefully. He looked left and right, darkness. He looked behind him, darkness.

Any small distraction, he needed one.

He unzipped it, making as little noise as possible.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"What in the pox is this?" He muttered with a puzzled expression, looking at some rounded cube-shapes made of a plastic-like material in an opaque brown color, along with the residue of some white powder that made his nose wrinkle.</p>

About one kilogram each, a weight he had memorized.

As it turned out, that plastic-looking surface was just the wrapping. But no matter how hard he tried, he could not break it open. He wiped the powder off on his trousers and quickly packed everything back. Though he planned to keep one of those cube-shapes, to ask Vicente about later.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"The fuck do you think you're doing?"</p>

An enormous figure emerged from the shadows, advancing violently toward Ciao, who was still seated on the ground.

The lights cut out at that exact moment. Not even the moonlight reached that place.

<p style="text-indent: 3em;">"Huh? Vicent—"</p>

Obviously it was not him. Twice Ciao's height and three times as wide; that was this figure.

The light returned in flashes, seconds of visibility, then darkness again. Even so, it was enough to make out a man. Ciao scrambled backward across the ground instinctively, trying not to lose sight of him.

In the lit moments, only the reflection in his eyes was visible.

A two-meter-tall monster.

Bald. His face tattooed entirely to resemble a skull, even his eyes were pitch black.

Flat on the ground, breathing in gasps, one of the blocks still gripped in his hand.

Then, everything went dark again...
